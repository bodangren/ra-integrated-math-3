# Product Guidelines: Math for Business Operations v2

## Experience Priorities

1. **Curriculum First**: the product exists to deliver classroom-ready business math, not to simulate a generic LMS.
2. **Student Clarity**: student pages must reduce ambiguity about where to start, what to do now, and what is complete.
3. **Teacher Signal**: teacher surfaces should emphasize progress, blockers, and next action over ornamental analytics.
4. **Pedagogical Restraint**: spreadsheets, simulations, and assessments should appear only when they clearly support the lesson objective.
5. **One Storyline**: curriculum docs, runtime content, activity labels, and teacher views must describe the same course in the same language.

## Voice and Tone

- Clear, direct, classroom-ready
- High-frequency vocabulary where possible
- Serious but not corporate
- Specific instructions over motivational filler

## Interaction Rules

- A student should always have a visible next step.
- A teacher should be able to identify student status without reading dense tables first.
- Progress language must distinguish **published curriculum** from drafts or planned content.
- Lesson shells must reflect lesson type instead of forcing every lesson into one UI pattern.

## Anti-Patterns

- No admin-first information architecture in phase 1
- No promise of in-app authoring before it exists
- No parallel curriculum story told by docs versus runtime
- No stale platform branding in active planning artifacts
