# Project Sprint: Class Presentation

## Phase 1: Presentation Setup
- **Goal:** Frame the day as a public presentation, not only a rehearsal.
- **Activity:** Review {{assets.presentation_rubric}} and the success criteria above. Teams confirm who will speak, who will control the workbook, and how the chart will be referenced.

## Phase 2: Final Preparation
- **Goal:** Give teams a short final prep window.
- **Activity:** Review {{assets.submission_checklist}}, fix last issues, and make sure the workbook and script are ready for live delivery.

## Phase 3: Class Presentation
- **Goal:** Present the completed product to the class.
- **Activity:** Each group presents its Balance Snapshot, explains the business's financial position, and answers at least one audience or teacher question using the workbook as evidence.

## Phase 4: Audience Feedback + Reflection
- **Goal:** Capture what students learned from public presentation.
- **Activity:** Audience members record one strength and one question for each team. Presenters then complete a short reflection on what evidence was strongest and what they would improve.

---

## Teacher Notes
- **Pedagogy:** Treat this as a performance lesson. The finished product is the workbook plus the explanation, not just a file upload.
- **Setup:** Ensure display logistics, timing, and audience-feedback forms are ready before class begins.
