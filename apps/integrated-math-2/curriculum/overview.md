# Integrated Math 2 Curriculum

## Overview

13 units, ~67 lessons covering geometry, algebra, trigonometry, and probability. Progression: geometric reasoning → algebraic modeling → advanced functions/trigonometry.

---

## Unit 1: Relationships in Triangles

*(Focus: Triangle properties, congruence, reasoning)*

### Lessons

- 1.1 Classifying Triangles (by sides and angles)
- 1.2 Triangle Angle Relationships
- 1.3 Triangle Inequality Theorem
- 1.4 Congruence Criteria (SSS, SAS, ASA, AAS, HL)
- 1.5 Proving Triangle Congruence
- 1.6 Isosceles & Equilateral Triangle Properties

### Core Objectives

- Classify triangles by sides and angles
- Apply triangle angle sum and exterior angle theorems
- Use triangle inequality to determine valid triangles
- Prove triangle congruence using multiple criteria
- Apply properties of isosceles and equilateral triangles

---

## Big Picture Course Objectives

Across all units, students are expected to:

- Develop **geometric reasoning** through proofs and constructions
- Build **algebraic fluency** with equations, functions, and polynomials
- Use **multiple representations** (graphs, tables, equations, words)
- Communicate reasoning using **mathematical language**
- Apply mathematics to **real-world modeling contexts**
- Develop **statistical and probabilistic thinking**
