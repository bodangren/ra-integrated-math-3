Angles can also be measured in units called radians, which are based
on arc length. A radian is the measure of an angle θ in standard
position with a terminal side that intercepts an arc with the same
length as the radius of the circle. The circumference of a circle is 2πr
and an angle that measures 1 radian has an arc length of r. So, one
complete revolution around a circle equals 2π radians.
